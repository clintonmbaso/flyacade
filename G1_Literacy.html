B1EngT1L01 – Greetings
Sunday, April 02, 2023

Objective:
- Students will learn basic greetings, including "hello" and "goodbye."
- Students will practice using these greetings in role-play scenarios with classmates.

Materials Needed:
1. Flashcards with images depicting greetings (e.g., smiling faces saying "hello" and "goodbye").
2. Whiteboard and markers.
3. Name tags for each student.
4. Props for role-play scenarios (optional but recommended).

Duration: Approximately 30-45 minutes

Lesson Outline:

Introduction (5 minutes):
1. Greet the students as they enter the classroom individually, saying "hello" to each one.
2. Gather the students in a circle and explain the objective of the lesson: to learn basic greetings like "hello" and "goodbye."
3. Show the flashcards with images of greetings and explain each one.

Vocabulary Introduction (10 minutes):
1. Write "hello" and "goodbye" on the whiteboard.
2. Pronounce each word clearly and have the students repeat after you several times.
3. Use gestures to reinforce the meaning of each word (e.g., waving while saying "hello" and waving while pretending to leave for "goodbye").

Role-play Practice (15 minutes):
1. Divide the class into pairs or small groups.
2. Assign each group a role-play scenario (e.g., meeting a friend at the park, saying goodbye to a family member before leaving for school).
3. Provide props if available to make the scenarios more engaging.
4. Encourage students to use the greetings they learned (hello and goodbye) during their role-play.
5. Circulate around the classroom, providing assistance and feedback as needed.

Review and Closing (5 minutes):
1. Gather the students back into the circle.
2. Ask a few volunteers to share their role-play scenarios with the class.
3. Review the greetings "hello" and "goodbye" one more time, emphasizing their importance in daily interactions.
4. End the lesson by saying "goodbye" to each student individually as they leave the classroom.

Homework (optional):
- Encourage students to practice using "hello" and "goodbye" with family members at home. They can also draw pictures or write about their experiences using these greetings.

Assessment:
- Informal assessment during role-play activities to observe students' understanding and usage of greetings.
- Participation and engagement during the lesson.
- Ability to correctly pronounce and use the vocabulary words "hello" and "goodbye" during interactions.

Materials Needed:
1. Flashcards with images depicting requests (e.g., asking for a toy, asking for help).
2. Whiteboard and markers.

4. Props for role-play scenarios (optional but recommended).

B1EngT1L06 – Counting Basics
Sunday, April 02, 2023

Objective:
- Students will learn numbers 1 through 5 and practice counting using fingers, objects, and simple exercises.

Materials Needed:
1. Flashcards with images depicting numbers 1 through 5.
2. Objects for counting (e.g., small toys, blocks).
3. Whiteboard and markers.
4. Name tags for each student.

Duration: Approximately 30-45 minutes

Lesson Outline:

Introduction (5 minutes):
1. Greet the students as they enter the classroom individually.
2. Gather the students in a circle and introduce the objective of the lesson: to learn numbers 1 through 5.
3. Show the flashcards with images depicting numbers 1 through 5 and briefly explain each number.

Number Introduction (10 minutes):
1. Write numbers 1 through 5 on the whiteboard.
2. Pronounce each number clearly and have the students repeat after you several times.
3. Demonstrate counting fingers from 1 to 5 and encourage students to count along with you.
4. Show examples of objects and count them together as a class, emphasizing each number.

Practice Activities (15 minutes):
1. Divide the class into pairs or small groups.
2. Distribute objects (toys, blocks, etc.) to each group.
3. Instruct students to take turns counting the objects in their group from 1 to 5.
4. Encourage students to use their fingers to represent each number as they count.
5. Circulate around the classroom, providing assistance and guidance as needed.

Interactive Exercises (10 minutes):
1. Conduct simple exercises where students have to count objects or actions.
2. For example, ask students to:
   - Count the number of chairs in the classroom.
   - Count the number of claps as you clap a certain number of times.
   - Count the number of jumps or hops they can do.
3. Encourage active participation and engagement from all students.

Review and Closing (5 minutes):
1. Review numbers 1 through 5 one more time, asking students to repeat after you.
2. Ask a few volunteers to demonstrate counting objects or fingers to the class.
3. End the lesson by expressing praise for students' efforts in learning numbers 1 through 5.

Homework (optional):
- Encourage students to practice counting objects or fingers at home with family members. They can also practice writing the numbers they learned.

Assessment:
- Informal assessment during practice activities and exercises to observe students' ability to count numbers 1 through 5 accurately.
- Participation and engagement during the lesson.
- Proficiency in recognizing and pronouncing numbers 1 through 5.
