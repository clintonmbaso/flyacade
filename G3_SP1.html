B1EngT1L01 – Introduction to Deductive Reasoning
Sunday, April 02, 2023

Objective:
- Understand the concept of deductive reasoning.
- Recognize the importance of deductive reasoning in daily life.

Materials:
- Whiteboard or flip chart
- Markers
- Picture cards with simple scenarios

Introduction:
1. Begin with a simple question: "What do you think deductive reasoning is?"
2. Discuss students' thoughts and ideas.
3. Define deductive reasoning: "Deductive reasoning is a way of thinking where you use general statements to reach specific conclusions."

Class Discussion:
4. Discuss the importance of deductive reasoning in everyday situations. Give examples like making decisions and solving problems.

Activity - Picture Cards:
5. Show picture cards with simple scenarios (e.g., a sunny day, a person holding an umbrella).
6. Ask students to deduce conclusions based on the pictures. For example, if it's sunny and someone has an umbrella, what can we conclude?

Guided Practice:
7. Guide students through a few examples on the whiteboard, reinforcing the deductive process.

Conclusion:
8. Summarize the lesson, emphasizing the role of deductive reasoning in making informed decisions.

B1EngT2L01 – Introduction to Conditional Statements (Day 1)
Sunday, April 02, 2023

Objective:
- Introduce the concept of conditional statements to Grade 3 students.

Introduction (10 mins):
1. Begin with a brief discussion on decision-making, asking students how they decide what activities to do based on different situations.
2. Introduce the idea of "conditional statements" by explaining that they are like rules that depend on certain conditions.

Guided Explanation (15 mins):
1. Use a relatable example: "If it's sunny, we go to the park."
2. Break down the statement: Discuss what the condition is (sunny weather) and what action follows (going to the park).
3. Write the example on the board, emphasizing the "If...then..." structure.

Interactive Activity (20 mins):
1. Engage students with a hands-on activity. Provide various weather condition cards and activity cards (e.g., park, library, stay indoors).
2. Ask students to create their conditional statements by matching weather conditions with appropriate activities.

Group Discussion (10 mins):
1. Have students share their created conditional statements with the class.
2. Discuss how different conditions lead to various actions and encourage questions.

Homework Assignment (5 mins):
Assign a simple task: Write a conditional statement using a condition from their daily life.
