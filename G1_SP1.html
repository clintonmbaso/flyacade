B1EngT1L01 – Introduction to Deductive Reasoning
Sunday, April 02, 2023

Objective:
- Introduce students to the concept of deductive reasoning.
- Develop an understanding of how logical conclusions can be drawn based on known information.

Introduction (15 minutes):
- Begin with a simple, relatable scenario (e.g., "All cats purr").
- Ask students to share what they know about cats and their behavior.

Activity 1: What Do We Know? (20 minutes):
- Provide pictures or drawings of various animals.
- Ask students to identify characteristics or behaviors they associate with each animal.
- Discuss as a class and identify common traits.

Mini-Lecture (15 minutes):
- Explain deductive reasoning using the example: "If all cats purr, and Fluffy is a cat, what can we conclude about Fluffy?"
- Emphasize the process of making logical connections based on existing knowledge.

Activity 2: Create Your Deductive Statements (25 minutes):
- Have students work in pairs.
- Provide them with different animal traits and ask them to create deductive statements (e.g., "All birds can fly").
- Each pair presents their statement to the class, and the class discusses the logical implications.

Conclusion (10 minutes):
- Summarize the key points of deductive reasoning.
- Assign a simple homework task, such as observing animals at home and noting behaviors.

B1EngT2L01 – Introduction to Conditional Statements
Sunday, April 02, 2023

Objective:
- Understand the concept of conditional statements.
- Recognize conditions and consequences.

Introduction (10 minutes):
1. Begin with a simple question: "Have you ever noticed that some things happen only when certain conditions are met?"
2. Explain that we are going to learn about something called "conditional statements" - sentences that describe actions based on specific conditions.

Definition and Discussion (15 minutes):
1. Define conditional statements using accessible language: "Conditional statements are like rules that tell us what to do when a certain thing happens."
2. Give examples outside of daily routines, such as "If it's raining, we use an umbrella" or "If it's sunny, we wear sunglasses."
3. Encourage students to share their thoughts on these examples.

Interactive Activity (15 minutes):
1. Provide simple scenarios and ask students to complete the conditional statements. For example, "If it's nighttime, we ____________."
2. Discuss their answers as a class, emphasizing the cause-and-effect relationship in each statement.

Wrap-up (5 minutes):
1. Recap what conditional statements are.
2. Ask students to think about one rule they follow at home and share it with the class.
