G2SDST1L01 – Exploring Different Names of God
Sunday, April 02, 2023

Objective:
- Students will learn and recognize different names attributed to the Christian God.
- Students will understand the significance and meanings associated with these names.

Materials:
1. Large poster with the names of God written in large, clear letters.
2. Whiteboard and markers.
3. Storybook or short passages from the Bible highlighting different names of God.
4. Small cards with individual names of God for a matching activity.
5. Coloring sheets related to the lesson.

Introduction (10 minutes):
1. Begin with a simple prayer, thanking God for the opportunity to learn about His names.
2. Show the poster with various names of God and discuss why names are important.

Activity 1: Storytelling (15 minutes):
1. Read a short story or passages from the Bible that mention different names of God (e.g., Jehovah, Elohim, Abba).
2. Pause and discuss each name, encouraging students to share what they think each name means.

Activity 2: Matching Game (10 minutes):
1. Distribute small cards with individual names of God to each student.
2. Have students match the cards with the corresponding names on the large poster.
3. Discuss the matches as a group, reinforcing the understanding of each name.

Activity 3: Art and Coloring (10 minutes):
1. Provide coloring sheets related to the names of God.
2. Encourage creativity and expression while coloring, fostering a connection to the lesson.

Discussion (5 minutes):
1. Facilitate a brief discussion on what the students learned about the names of God.
2. Ask if they have any questions or if there's a particular name they found interesting.

Conclusion (5 minutes):
1. Summarize the key points of the lesson.
2. End with a short prayer, expressing gratitude for the understanding of God's different names.

Homework Assignment (optional):
Ask students to talk to their families about the lesson and share if they know any additional names for God used in their homes.

G1EngT1L01 – Understanding the Significance of Traditional
Sunday, April 02, 2023

Objective:
- Students will learn about the significance of traditional ceremonies, focusing on the concepts of identity, unity, and happiness.
- Students will understand how traditional ceremonies contribute to cultural identity, community unity, and the joy of celebration.

Materials:
1. Chart paper and markers
2. Picture cards illustrating traditional ceremonies
3. World map or globe
4. Storybooks about the significance of traditional ceremonies
5. Drawing paper and crayons

Introduction (15 minutes):
1. Begin with a discussion about the concept of cultural traditions and why they are important.
2. Show the location of different countries on the world map or globe to highlight cultural diversity.
3. Introduce the idea that traditional ceremonies play a crucial role in shaping cultural identity, fostering unity, and bringing happiness to communities.

Activity 1: Charting Significance (20 minutes):
1. Use chart paper to create three charts labeled "Identity," "Unity," and "Happiness."
2. Discuss with the class how traditional ceremonies contribute to cultural identity, community unity, and the joy of celebration.
3. Record their responses on the respective charts.

Activity 2: Picture Cards (15 minutes):
1. Use picture cards to illustrate traditional ceremonies from various cultures.
2. Discuss each picture card with the class, asking students to identify how the ceremonies contribute to identity, unity, and happiness.
3. Record key points on the Identity, Unity, and Happiness charts.

Activity 3: Class Discussion (20 minutes):
1. Engage the class in a discussion about the significance of traditional ceremonies in preserving cultural identity.
2. Discuss how these ceremonies bring communities together, fostering a sense of unity and togetherness.
3. Explore the idea that traditional celebrations often bring happiness and joy to individuals and communities.

Activity 4: Storytime (15 minutes):
1. Read storybooks that highlight the significance of traditional ceremonies in different cultures.
2. Discuss the characters' experiences and how the ceremonies contribute to their identity, unity, and happiness.
3. Relate the stories to the broader themes of cultural significance and the positive impact of traditions.

Activity 5: Drawing and Reflection (15 minutes):
1. Provide drawing paper and crayons.
2. Ask students to draw a picture representing a traditional ceremony that is important to them or their community, focusing on identity, unity, or happiness.
3. Allow each student to share their drawing and briefly describe the elements they included.

Conclusion (10 minutes):
1. Summarize key points about the significance of traditional ceremonies in fostering identity, unity, and happiness.
2. Emphasize the importance of respecting and understanding diverse cultural traditions.
3. Encourage a class discussion on how traditional ceremonies contribute to the richness of cultural heritage and strengthen community bonds.

Assessment:
- Participation in class discussions and activities.
- Creativity and understanding demonstrated through drawings and contributions to the discussion.

Note: Ensure sensitivity to diverse cultural backgrounds within the classroom. Foster an environment that values and respects the various traditions associated with traditional ceremonies and encourages an understanding of their significance in promoting identity, unity, and happiness.
