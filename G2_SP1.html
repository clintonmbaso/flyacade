B1EngT1L01 – Introduction to Deductive Reasoning
Sunday, April 02, 2023

Objective:
- Understand the concept of deductive reasoning.
- Apply deductive reasoning in simple scenarios.

Introduction:
1. Begin by asking the students if they have ever tried to solve a puzzle or figure out a mystery.
2. Explain that deductive reasoning is like being a detective – it's about making smart guesses based on the clues we have.

Class Discussion:
1. Discuss familiar daily activities. For example, ask students what they do before leaving for school or going to bed.
2. Encourage them to think about the logical order of these activities and why they do certain things in a particular sequence.

Activity 1: Sequencing Cards
1. Provide cards with pictures of daily activities (e.g., brushing teeth, putting on shoes).
2. Ask students to arrange the cards in the correct sequence, explaining why they placed each card where they did.

Activity 2: Mystery Bag
1. Place various items in a bag.
2. Take an item out and discuss its characteristics. Ask students to guess what other items might be in the bag based on deductive reasoning.

Conclusion:
1. Summarize deductive reasoning as making logical guesses based on information.
2. Discuss how deductive reasoning is part of our everyday lives.

B1EngT2L01 – Introduction to Conditional Statements
Sunday, April 02, 2023

Objective:
- Understand the concept of conditional statements.

1. Introduction (15 minutes):
   - Begin with a discussion on making choices based on different situations.
   - Introduce the term "conditional statements" and explain that they are rules we follow depending on specific conditions.

2. Definition and Examples (20 minutes):
   - Define conditional statements in simple terms suitable for grade 2.
   - Provide examples like "If it's sunny, wear sunglasses" and discuss why we wear sunglasses when it's sunny.

3. Class Activity - Sorting (15 minutes):
   - Prepare pictures or cards illustrating various weather conditions and corresponding actions (e.g., rain, snow, sun).
   - Have students work in pairs to match the conditions with appropriate actions, reinforcing the concept.

4. Discussion (10 minutes):
   - Engage in a class discussion about the activity, encouraging students to share their reasoning for matching specific conditions with actions.

5. Homework Assignment (Optional):
   - Ask students to draw a picture or write a sentence about a conditional statement they follow at home.
