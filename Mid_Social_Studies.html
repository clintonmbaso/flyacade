M2SStT1L01 - Name
Wednesday, April 05, 2023

Methodology

- Introduce the lesson by coming up with a game that will lead the learners to say their names e.g. “do you know my name”
- Tell the learners your name and ask them to say their names e.g. “my name is Mary, what is your name?”
Draw in the learners books or paper a picture of a boy or girl.
- Sing a song on self-introduction.
- Pair learners and allow them to ask each other their names.

Activity

- In pairs learners to ask each other their names.
- Learners to sing a song on self introduction.
- Colour a picture of a boy or a girl.

Assessment

Listen as learners say their names.

M2SStT1L04 - Likes and Dislikes
Wednesday, April 05, 2023

Methodology

- Revise the previous lesson on mentioning learners’ ages to each other.
- Introduce the lesson by asking learners leading questions such as: ‘I like eating mangoes’, ‘what do you like?’
- Allow learners to mention their likes such as ‘I like eating chocolate’,
- Ask learners what they do not like, ‘I do not like waking up in the morning’.
- Show picture of learners in the learner’s book page 3 and ask them to talk about their likes and dislikes.

Activity

- Learners to describe themselves, and what they like or dislike in pairs.
- Learners to circle a picture of what they like in their books.
- Learners to circle a picture of what they dislike in their books.

Assessment

Listen as learners talk about their likes and dislikes.

M2SStT1L05 - Friends
Wednesday, April 05, 2023

Methodology

- Revise the previous lesson on talking about their likes and dislikes.
- Introduce the lesson by asking questions like: ‘Do you have friends?’
- Tell a story about two good friends e.g. James and Jane while learners will listen attentively.
- Ask learners to identify friends among themselves after the story.
- Show learners a picture of friends in the learner’s book page 4.
- Demonstrate the exercise to the learners for colouring the drawing of learners playing together as friends in a clear view.
- Encourage those who did not do well to repeat the exercise.
- Praise the learners for their participation.

Activity

- Learners to listen to the story.
- Learners to point at the picture of friends shown in the book.
- Learners to colour pictures of learners playing together as friends.

Assessment

- Listen to learners as they identify their friends.
- Observe learners as they colour pictures of friends playing

M2SStT2L05 - Cleaning own items

Methodology

 Introduce the lesson by revising the previous lesson on school.
 Display the learning and teaching material in a clear view of the learners (toys)
 Ask the learners to identify the toys and mention how they play with them.
 Ask all learners to contribute and participate.
 Tell learners about the importance of caring for their own toys.
 Give the learners time to play with the toys and tell the learners about the concept of tiding up.

Activity

 Learners identify the toys
 Learners play with the toys
 Learners to wind up their play activity
 Learners to mention, how they care for their toys
 Learners tidy up their toys and put them in the right place.

Assessment

Observe the learners as they care for their toys and tide up.
