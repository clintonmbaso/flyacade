Moon Phases

The Moon doesn't produce its own light. We see the Moon because it reflects light from the Sun. As the Moon orbits Earth, we see different portions of its illuminated half, creating the phases of the Moon.

Eclipses

Eclipses occur when one celestial body moves into the shadow of another. There are two main types: solar eclipses and lunar eclipses.

Solar Eclipse

A solar eclipse occurs when the Moon passes between the Sun and Earth, blocking all or part of the Sun's light.

Lunar Eclipse

A lunar eclipse occurs when Earth comes between the Sun and the Moon, causing Earth's shadow to fall on the Moon.

Shadow Types

Umbra: The darkest part of the shadow where light is completely blocked.
Penumbra: The lighter part of the shadow where light is only partially blocked.
