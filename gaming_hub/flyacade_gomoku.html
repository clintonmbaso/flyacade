How to Play Gomoku

Gomoku, also known as Five in a Row, is a classic strategy board game. The objective is simple but requires deep strategic thinking.

  • The game is played on a 15×15 grid board.
  • Black plays first, followed by White, alternating turns.
  • Players place their stones on empty intersections.
  • The first player to form an unbroken line of five stones horizontally, vertically, or diagonally wins.
  • There are no captures - once placed, stones remain until the game ends.

Pro Tip: Try to create multiple threats simultaneously while blocking your opponent's attempts!

Advanced Strategies

  • Control the Center: The center of the board offers the most opportunities for creating winning lines.
  • Create Multiple Threats: Try to set up positions where you have two potential winning lines that your opponent can't block simultaneously.
  • Block and Attack: Always respond to your opponent's threats while advancing your own position.
  • Think Ahead: Consider not just your next move, but how the board will look 2-3 moves ahead.
  • Use the "Four in a Row" Trap: Setting up an unblocked four in a row forces your opponent to respond, often creating opportunities elsewhere.
