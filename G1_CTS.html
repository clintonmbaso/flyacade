Types of Lines
There are various types of lines used in art and design, each serving a different purpose and creating a unique visual effect. Here are details on some common types of lines:

1. Straight Line: A straight line is simple and unbroken, extending from one point to another without curves. It can convey stability, order, and precision.
Examples: Ruler-edge lines, the edge of a book.

2. Curved Line: A curved line is gentle and flows smoothly, often forming a continuous curve. It can suggest grace, movement, and fluidity.
Examples: Circles, arcs, and waves.

Examples: Lightning bolts, jagged edges.

Examples: Dashed lines, broken road markings.

Proper food storage is essential to maintain food quality, prevent spoilage, and ensure food safety. It involves storing different types of food at the correct temperatures and using suitable containers to minimize the risk of contamination. Here are details on food storage:

1. Freezing: Freeze foods that you don't plan to use immediately, such as meats, vegetables, and bread. Package items in airtight containers or freezer bags to prevent freezer burn. Label packages with the date of freezing to keep track of freshness.

2. Dry Storage: Dry storage is suitable for non-perishable items like grains, cereals, canned goods, and dry pasta. Keep these foods in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and moisture.

G1CTST3L14 – MEASURING
Sunday, April 02, 2023
1. Dry Ingredients: For dry ingredients like flour, sugar, and spices, use dry measuring cups. Fill the cup to overflowing and level it off with a flat edge (e.g., a knife) for an accurate measurement. Use a straight-edged spatula or knife to level the dry ingredients in the cup without tapping or shaking the cup.

2. Liquid Ingredients: For liquids like water, milk, or oil, use liquid measuring cups. Fill the cup to the desired mark at eye level for an accurate measurement. Check the meniscus (the curved surface of the liquid) to ensure an accurate measurement.
