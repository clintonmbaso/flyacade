Sunday, April 02, 2023
Explore basic shapes like circles, squares, and triangles through drawing and crafting. Practice drawing simple objects like houses, trees, and animals using these shapes. Develop hand-eye coordination and fine motor skills through coloring and tracing.

Basic Shapes:

1. Circle: A circle is a perfectly round shape with no corners or edges. It is often found in objects like wheels, coins, and balls.

2. Square: A square has four equal sides and four right angles. It is commonly seen in objects like books, windows, and picture frames.

3. Triangle: A triangle has three sides and three angles. It can come in various forms, such as equilateral (all sides and angles are equal), isosceles (two sides and two angles are equal), or scalene (no sides or angles are equal).

4. Rectangle: A rectangle has four sides with opposite sides being equal in length and four right angles. Common objects like doors, screens, and paper are often rectangular.

G1CTST1L04 – Drawing Basic Shapes
Sunday, April 02, 2023
How to Draw Basic Shapes:
Drawing basic shapes is an essential skill for young learners. Here's how to draw each of them:

1. Circle: Start by drawing a point in the center, then draw lines or curves around it, keeping the distance from the center point equal.

2. Square: Draw four equal sides connected by four right angles. You can start by drawing one side and then adding the other three.

3. Triangle: Begin with a straight line for the base. From each endpoint of the base, draw lines slanting towards each other to create the triangle.

4. Rectangle: Draw two equal pairs of parallel lines, with each pair being opposite to the other. Ensure that the angles are right angles.

G1CTST1L06 – DRAWING
Sunday, April 02, 2023
Practice drawing simple objects and animals using basic shapes and lines.

Drawing Simple Objects Using Basic Shapes and Lines:

1. Select an Object: Choose a simple object you want to draw, such as an apple, a cup, or a ball.

2. Analyze the Object: Look closely at the object and try to break it down into basic shapes. Identify the key shapes that make up the object.

3. Start with Basic Shapes: Begin your drawing by lightly sketching the basic shapes that represent the object's main components. For example, if you're drawing an apple, start with a circle for the apple's body and an oval for the stem.

4. Refine the Details: Once you have the basic shapes in place, start adding more details using lines. Pay attention to any curves, edges, or patterns on the object, and use lines to represent these features.

5. Erase and Adjust
Don't worry if your initial lines and shapes aren't perfect. Use an eraser to refine your drawing, adjusting the proportions and shapes as needed.

6. Add Shadows and Shading (Optional): To make your object appear more three-dimensional, you can add shading to create shadows and highlights. Shade areas of the object that would be in shadow and leave others lighter to show highlights.

7. Finalize Your Drawing: Once you're satisfied with your drawing, go over your lines with darker, more defined lines to make your object stand out.

Importance of Family Meals:  Family meals offer a chance for family members to come together, connect, and share experiences.  They provide a platform for open communication, where family members can discuss their day, concerns, and achievements. Regular family meals can positively influence children's development, academic performance, and emotional well-being. They promote healthy eating habits by offering balanced and nutritious meals.

Planning Family Meals: Plan family meals ahead to accommodate everyone's schedules. Choose a time that works for all family members as often as possible. Involve family members in meal planning and allow them to contribute ideas and preferences. Consider dietary restrictions or preferences when planning meals.
