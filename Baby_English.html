B1EngT1L01 – Exploring the "Ah" Sound Adventure
Sunday, April 02, 2023

Objective:
Introduce and reinforce the "ah" sound through engaging activities and games.

Introduction:
1. Begin with a short video featuring adorable animals like a cat, bat, and mat, accompanied by playful music emphasizing the "ah" sound.
2. Share a simple story about a friendly cat, wearing a hat, playing with a bat on a mat. Emphasize the "ah" sound in each word.

Warm-Up:
1. Ask the children to repeat after you as you say words like "cat," "hat," "bat," and "mat," emphasizing the "ah" sound.
2. Use flashcards with pictures of these objects to make it visually engaging.

Main Activity - Sound Recognition Game:
1. Read a short story or sing a song containing words with the "ah" sound.
2. Instruct students to raise their hands every time they hear a word with the "ah" sound.
3. Use interactive elements like puppets or visuals to keep their attention.

Hands-On Practice:
1. Distribute coloring sheets with images of a cat, hat, bat, and mat. Encourage children to color while repeating the words.
2. Use playdough to create simple shapes related to the story, emphasizing the "ah" sound.

Closing:
1. Recap the words learned, asking each child to share their favorite "ah" sound word from the lesson.
2. End with a fun goodbye song, incorporating the "ah" sound.

Extension Activity (Optional):
Provide parents with a list of "ah" sound words to reinforce learning at home.

Materials:
- Short video featuring "ah" sound words
- Flashcards with pictures
- Storybook or song lyrics
- Coloring sheets
- Playdough
- Puppets or visuals for the sound recognition game

Adjust the pace and activities based on the age and attention span of the baby class, ensuring a balance between structured learning and play.

B1EngT1L02 – Ah-Some Word Adventure
Sunday, April 02, 2023

Objective:
Reinforce the "ah" sound through practice, pronunciation, and word-building games.

Introduction:
1. Begin by introducing a list of words containing the "ah" sound: "bag," "sand," "jam," "sad."
2. Pronounce each word, emphasizing the "ah" sound, and encourage students to repeat after you.

Pronunciation and Writing Practice:
1. Provide each student with a mini-whiteboard and markers.
2. Ask students to write the introduced words while pronouncing them aloud.
3. Offer individual assistance as needed, ensuring each student practices both writing and pronunciation.

Word-Building Game:
1. Prepare letter cards with vowels, consonants, and specifically, cards with the letters 'a' and 'h.'
2. Demonstrate how to create "ah" sound words by combining letter cards.
3. Let students take turns picking cards and arranging them to form words containing the "ah" sound.
4. Encourage them to read out their created words and celebrate their success.

Interactive Activity - "Ah" Sound Bingo:
1. Create Bingo cards with pictures or words featuring the "ah" sound.
2. Call out words randomly, and students mark the corresponding word or picture on their Bingo cards.
3. Celebrate with small prizes or praise for those who complete a row.

Closing:
1. Recap the words practiced during the lesson, asking students to share their favorite "ah" sound word.
2. End with a short rhyme or song incorporating the "ah" sound.

Materials:
- List of "ah" sound words (e.g., "bag," "sand," "jam," "sad")
- Mini-whiteboards and markers
- Letter cards (including 'a' and 'h')
- Bingo cards with "ah" sound words
- Small prizes or stickers for the Bingo game

Note: Adjust the complexity of the word-building game based on the students' proficiency levels. This lesson plan encourages active participation and engagement while reinforcing the "ah" sound in a variety of ways.

B1EngT1L04 – Reading and Writing Adventure with "Ah" Sounds
Sunday, October 22, 2023

Objective:
Enhance reading and writing skills by exploring a short story or poem containing words with the "ah" sound.

Introduction:
1. Start by discussing the "ah" sound and its presence in various words.
2. Introduce the short story or poem, capturing students' attention with the promise of discovering "ah" sounds within.

Reading Activity:
1. Read the selected short story or poem, emphasizing words with the "ah" sound.
2. Pause at certain points to ask students if they heard any words with the targeted sound.

1. Provide each student with paper and writing tools.

3. Encourage creativity and offer guidance as needed.

Sharing and Peer Interaction:
1. Allow students to share their written paragraphs or sentences with the class.
2. Encourage positive feedback and discussion about the different ways the "ah" sound was used.

Extension Activity (Optional):
Assign a simple at-home writing task where students can create a short story or poem using words with the "ah" sound.

Closing:
1. Summarize the key words and concepts learned during the reading and writing activities.
2. Express enthusiasm for the students' creativity and engagement in the lesson.

Materials:
- Short story or poem with "ah" sound words
- Writing tools (paper, pencils, etc.)
- Board or shared document for word identification

This lesson plan integrates reading and writing skills, providing students with an immersive experience in identifying and using words with the "ah" sound.
