R03MathT1L01 – Introducing Numbers 1-3
Sunday, April 02, 2023

Objective: Introduce kindergarten learners to numbers 1-3 and counting objects and fingers.

Materials Needed:
- Counting objects (e.g., blocks, toys)
- Chart with numbers 1-3
- Finger puppets or illustrations of fingers

Activities:
1. Warm-up (5 minutes): Gather students in a circle and sing a counting song, such as "Five Little Ducks."
2. Introduction (10 minutes): Show the chart with numbers 1-3. Have students repeat each number after you.
3. Counting Objects (15 minutes): Distribute counting objects to each student. Guide them in counting each object aloud.
4. Finger Counting (10 minutes): Use finger puppets or illustrations of fingers to demonstrate counting from 1 to 3. Encourage students to practice counting their own fingers.

Assessment: Have students count objects independently, and observe their ability to correctly identify numbers 1-3.

Introducing Numbers 1-5
Monday, March 11, 2024

Objective: Introduce kindergarten learners to numbers 1-5 and practice recognizing and writing them.

Materials Needed:
- Number flashcards or chart displaying numbers 1-5
- Writing materials (paper, pencils, markers)

Activities:
1. Introduction (10 minutes): Show number flashcards or display a chart with numbers 1-5. Lead students in repeating each number after you.
2. Recognition Practice (15 minutes): Hold up flashcards with numbers 1-5 randomly and ask students to identify each number aloud. Repeat until students are familiar with the numbers.
3. Writing Practice (15 minutes): Provide each student with a piece of paper and writing materials. Guide them in tracing and then writing each number 1-5.
4. Game (10 minutes): Play a simple game where students take turns finding and pointing to numbers 1-5 on the chart or flashcards.

Assessment: Observe students' ability to recognize and write numbers 1-5 during the writing practice activity.

G1EngT1L01 – Introduction to Addition with Sums up to 2
Sunday, April 02, 2023

Objective: Introduce kindergarten learners to simple addition with sums up to 2.

Materials Needed:
- Counting objects (e.g., blocks, toys)
- Number flashcards (0-2)
- Writing materials

Activities:
1. Introduction (10 minutes): Show number flashcards 0-2 and briefly explain addition as combining two groups to find the total.
2. Demonstration (15 minutes): Use counting objects to demonstrate simple addition problems such as 1 + 1 and 2 + 0. Encourage students to count aloud as they combine the groups.
3. Guided Practice (15 minutes): Provide students with addition worksheets featuring sums up to 2. Guide them through solving each problem, emphasizing counting and combining groups.
4. Independent Practice (10 minutes): Distribute additional worksheets for students to complete independently. Circulate the room to offer assistance as needed.

Assessment: Observe students' ability to correctly solve addition problems with sums up to 2 during guided and independent practice.

Applying Addition and Subtraction in Real-life Scenarios
Monday, March 11, 2024

Objective: Apply addition and subtraction skills to solve real-life problems.

Materials Needed:
- Picture cards depicting real-life scenarios (e.g., fruits at a market, toys on a shelf)
- Writing materials
- Addition and subtraction word problem worksheets

Activities:
1. Introduction (10 minutes): Introduce addition and subtraction word problems using picture cards depicting real-life scenarios. Discuss how addition involves combining quantities, while subtraction involves taking away or finding the difference.
2. Guided Practice (20 minutes): Present addition and subtraction word problems to the class, using the picture cards as visual aids. Guide students through solving each problem step-by-step.
3. Independent Practice (15 minutes): Distribute addition and subtraction word problem worksheets. Allow students to work independently, using the picture cards as references if needed.
4. Discussion (10 minutes): Lead a class discussion about how addition and subtraction can be used in everyday situations, and encourage students to share their own experiences.

Assessment: Monitor students' ability to apply addition and subtraction skills to solve real-life problems during guided and independent practice, as well as during the class discussion.
